# Claim Boundary

## Allowed claims

- This repository accompanies an AI-assisted independent research manuscript on A10-STMS.
- A10-STMS is a nondimensional reduced-surrogate audit of hybrid thermal control.
- The framework tests separated-barrier control for thermal safety and mission throughput under radiative, storage, power, delay, and actuator-authority constraints.
- The final locked status reported in the paper is PASS-PHASE2E-DELAY-DEGRADE-ROBUSTNESS.
- The final claim is limited to tested high-resource manageable, delayed-observation, moderate actuator-degradation, and out-of-sample stress families.

## Forbidden claims

- This is a validated spacecraft thermal-control technology.
- This has been tested in thermal vacuum.
- This is a spacecraft hardware design.
- This is a flight-ready controller.
- This proves superiority over existing spacecraft thermal-control architectures.
- This introduces a new heat-transfer mechanism.
- This solves harsh combined stress.
- This provides a formal control-barrier-function proof.

## Standard short wording

> A10-STMS is a nondimensional reduced-surrogate audit of mission-variable-preserving hybrid thermal control. It is not a spacecraft-ready cooling system, thermal-vacuum-validated technology, or flight controller.
